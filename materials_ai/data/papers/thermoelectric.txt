Bismuth telluride (Bi2Te3) is the state-of-the-art thermoelectric material for near-room-temperature applications. Bi2Te3 exhibits a high Seebeck coefficient combined with low lattice thermal conductivity, resulting in a thermoelectric figure of merit ZT approximately 1.0 at 300K. Spark plasma sintering has been demonstrated as an effective method for consolidating Bi2Te3-based thermoelectric materials. The material is primarily used in thermoelectric generators for waste heat recovery. Other promising thermoelectric materials include SnSe with a ZT of 2.6 at 923K, PbTe doped with Na, and skutterudite compounds such as CoSb3. Half-Heusler alloys like ZrNiSn also show potential for high-temperature thermoelectric applications.
