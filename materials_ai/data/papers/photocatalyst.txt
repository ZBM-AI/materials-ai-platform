Titanium dioxide (TiO2) is the most widely studied photocatalyst due to its chemical stability, low cost, and excellent photocatalytic activity. TiO2 in the anatase phase has a band gap of approximately 3.2 eV, limiting its photoactivity to the UV region. Various strategies including doping with nitrogen or transition metals and heterojunction formation with other semiconductors such as CdS and g-C3N4 have been explored to extend its absorption into the visible range. TiO2 photocatalysts are commonly synthesized by sol-gel or hydrothermal methods and are used for water splitting and environmental remediation. ZnO with a band gap of 3.37 eV is another prominent photocatalyst, especially in nanostructured forms such as nanorods and nanowires. BiVO4 with a band gap of 2.4 eV has emerged as a visible-light-active photocatalyst for water oxidation.
