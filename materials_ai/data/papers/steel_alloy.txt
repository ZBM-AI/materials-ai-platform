The equiatomic FeCoNiCrMn high-entropy alloy, also known as the Cantor alloy, exhibits a single-phase face-centered cubic solid solution structure. The alloy demonstrates an excellent combination of yield strength exceeding 350 MPa and ductility, particularly at cryogenic temperatures when tensile tested at 77K. Arc melting is the most common fabrication method for HEAs. FeCoNiCrMn shows great potential as a structural material in cryogenic and high-temperature applications due to its remarkable phase stability. Other high-entropy alloys such as AlCoCrFeNi with a BCC structure and refractory HEAs like MoNbTaWV have been developed for different property requirements. Precipitation-hardened HEAs with added Al and Ti show yield strengths exceeding 1 GPa through the formation of L12 precipitates.
