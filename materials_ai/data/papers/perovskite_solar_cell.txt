Organic-inorganic hybrid perovskites, particularly methylammonium lead iodide (MAPbI3), have emerged as revolutionary materials for photovoltaic applications. MAPbI3 with a direct band gap of 1.55 eV adopts the tetragonal perovskite crystal structure at room temperature. The material is typically deposited by spin coating or chemical vapor deposition. Power conversion efficiencies exceeding 25% have been demonstrated in perovskite solar cells. The most commonly used electron transport layer materials are TiO2 and SnO2, while spiro-OMeTAD serves as the hole transport layer. Recent research has focused on improving the stability of MAPbI3 against moisture and thermal degradation by using mixed-cation compositions such as CsFAMAPbI3.
