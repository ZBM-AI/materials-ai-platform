Lithium cobalt oxide (LiCoO2) remains the dominant cathode material in commercial lithium-ion batteries. LiCoO2 exhibits a layered crystal structure with a theoretical specific capacity of 274 mAh/g. The material is typically synthesized by co-precipitation followed by high-temperature solid-state reaction. Despite its widespread use, alternatives like NMC (LiNiMnCoO2) and LFP (LiFePO4) are being developed for improved thermal stability. Sodium-ion batteries using Na3V2(PO4)3 cathode material have also attracted attention as a lower-cost alternative. Solid-state electrolytes such as LLZO (Li7La3Zr2O12) with garnet structure show high ionic conductivity and are promising for all-solid-state batteries.
